# Contributing to DA·ON Revolution

Welcome. The fact that you are reading this means something brought you here —
a Brief, a conversation, a frustration with how human groups fail to hold together,
or a conviction that they don't have to.

That is enough. Read on.

---

## How This Project Works

DA·ON Revolution is governed by the same principle it is building:
the **ARA Axiom** — Authority, Responsibility, and Accountability
must be clearly mapped in every act of delegation.

We do not have a project manager. We do not have a CEO.
We have a Mission, a set of Protocols, and people who choose
to take responsibility for specific parts of what needs to be done.

Until the DA·ON software exists to automate this, we use
**GitHub Issues as a human-operated governance layer**:

- A contributor senses that something needs to happen
- They open an Issue describing the question, the context, and the stakes
- The community deliberates in the thread
- A path forward is agreed upon
- Someone takes the ADP-License for the work (names themselves as responsible)
- The work gets done and the Issue closes

This is the XDP in manual mode. It is intentionally temporary.
When the software exists, it will govern itself.

## What We Are Building Right Now

The immediate priority is the **MVP specification** —
translating the ADP and XDP Protocols into a software architecture
that a developer can build from.

If you can contribute to this, open an Issue titled:
`[ARCHITECTURE] Initial MVP Specification — [your name]`

Describe your background and what you see as the first question
that needs answering. The community will engage from there.

## What You Receive

No equity is offered. The absence of equity is not a limitation —
it is the integrity of the project.

A tool designed to eliminate hierarchy cannot be governed by
a capitalistic ownership structure. To offer equity would be to
become the thing we are building to replace.

What you receive instead:

- **Attribution** — your name in the permanent record of every contribution,
  in all publications, in the repository history
- **Founding Cohort Rights** — full governance participation under DA·ON,
  the same egalitarian standing as every other member
- **First Access** — priority access to all Animiatry services, Academy training,
  and DA·ON deployments as they become available
- **Social Capital** — the compounding value of being visibly associated
  with this work at the moment it is most needed

## The First Step

**Open an Issue and introduce yourself.**

Title it: `[INTRO] — [your name] — [what brought you here]`

Tell us:
- What you do and what skills you bring
- What aspect of the project resonates most
- What you want to contribute or understand better

Someone will respond. That conversation is the beginning
of your ADP-License — the definition of what you take
responsibility for in this Collective.

## On the Language We Use

A few terms that appear throughout this project carry specific meanings.
If something seems unusual, it is probably intentional:

- **Collective** not organization — people are not objects to be organized
- **Members** not users — we are participants, not consumers
- **Contribution** not employment — we give to a commons, not sell to an employer
- **Mission-Tree** — the living network of delegations that grows from the Root Mission
- **ADP-License** — the agreement that maps Authority, Responsibility, and Accountability
  for a specific piece of work

The full glossary lives in the [OZI-ology knowledge base](https://wordsofwizdom.substack.com).

## Code of Conduct

One principle governs conduct in this project:

> *What matters to you matters to me.*

This is the definition of Respect in OZI-ology. It does not mean agreement.
It means acknowledgment — that your experience, your perspective,
and your contribution are seen and given weight.

When this principle is violated, an Issue is the appropriate place to name it.
The community addresses it through the same deliberative process used for everything else.
